The Science of Prospective Memory

Prospective memory is the ability to remember to perform an action at a future time. One recalling facts, remember the future environment is to translate recalling between actions and remembering future actions, thus can remembering future action to avoids.

In daily life, many of these tasks are small, repeated, and easy to lose: did I already take my medication, do I still need to send that message, did I lock the car, did I finish today's habit?

The Problem: Fleeting vs. Persistent Cues

These tasks are vulnerable because they compete with whatever currently has your attention. If the cue is weak, delayed, or buried in a long notification list, the intention can disappear.

Why External Cues Help

An external cue reduces the amount of memory work your brain has to do. Instead of rehearsing the intention internally, you offload it into the environment.

Visible

Easier to re-check than a fleeting thought, external visual cues provide a physical signal.

Persistent

Better than one-time alerts. Wrist cues offer a continuous signal, reducing cognitive load.

At Point of Action

Better than early cues. Reminders are most effective when visible during the action moment.
